c1 Reflection Blog

  • What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?
    • Based on what I have heard, read and watched (fireside chat video), I am excited about the opportunity to learn in a non-traditional learning environment. I like the self-taking responsibility part.

  • What are your impressions?
    • I have been in this environment (phase 0) for about 3 days at present, and I understand how more work might make one feel under-pressure. So far some things have been challenging, but as someone said about writing software: "95% of the time it won't work". The sense of achievement comes from the finished product, and the incremental steps in getting there.

  • How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?
    • I am liking the sharing and supportive nature of other people. I have also tried to help others out when I can. I have already learnt many new 'tricks' in various areas as a result. In my 'current life' people ask me questions a lot of times, and I feel good in helping them. Even though software development is new to me, I would like to carry through my streak of 'wanting to contribute' to the EDA environment as much as possible.

  • Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?
    • If Shereef's video is anything to go by, I would definitely want to do my best. He talks about getting as much out of this as one puts in; which is a very good comment. I'm usually 'independently-creative' so asking for help can be difficult for me, but on the other hand I will implement my brand of self-responsibility, creativity & independence in the most productive way possible.

  • Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?
    • I am excited about being able to participate in this non-traditional environment of learning. I have heard only positive feedback about this type of a setup, and wish to do my best at working hard, accepting information about new-to-me technology, and processes, and making the most from this experience. Of-course there is an element of nervousness that comes into play, but I guess it also helps keep one's faculties sharp haha. Choose courage over comfort, as they say.
